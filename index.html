Who is Lady Gaga?

Lady Gaga is an award winning singer, songwriter, actress, and multihyphenate artist. Gaga is known as the “Queen of Pop” and has changed the face of the music and entertainment industry through her incredible career. Outside of her music, Lady Gaga advocates for LGBTQ rights, she founded a non-profit organization for youth empowerment, and has fundraised for the relief of natural disasters throughout her career. Gaga is known for her outlandish fashion sense and the bold aesthetic of her concerts, music videos, and performances.

Early Life

Stefani Joanna Angelina Germanotta, Lady Gaga, was born into an upper-middle class catholic family in Lenox Hill, New York in 1986. She began playing the piano at four years old and went on to perform in Regis High School’s musicals. She studied acting at the Lee Strasberg Theatre and Film Institute and music at NYU’s music program, CAP 21 in 2003. In 2005, she dropped out of school to pursue music full time.

Gaga’s Hit Career

Lady Gaga got her start playing small music gigs in clubs on the Lower East Side of Manhattan. After her singles “Just Dance” and “Poker Face” hit number one on global charts, her debut album, The Fame, in 2008, took the world by storm. Between 2011 and 2014, she produced her second album Born This Way, her third album Artpop, and worked on a Jazz album with Tony Benett called Cheek to Cheek. In 2016, she produced her fourth solo album Joanne, named for her late aunt. In 2018, she starred opposite Bradley Cooper in the remake of the movie A Star is Born, in which she wrote the hit song “Shallow.” In 2020, she released Chromatica and this past spring, she released Mayhem. She is currently on The Mayhem Ball tour, performing live in front of audiences across the globe.
